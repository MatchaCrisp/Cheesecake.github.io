Thousands of years ago, culinary geniuses in ancient Greece created the cheesecake, or rather, the ancestor to what we come to know as the modern cheesecake. For the ancient recipe calls for the pounding of flour and honey into patties of cheese, which one can imagine has a fairly different flavor profile to what cheesecake has become. The love for this dessert and cheese in general persisted through history. It found popularity in several cultures, notable among them, the British. Therefore when the Americas were colonized, the dessert came along. Now up until this point the dessert has evolved to be more intricate in preparation, more stringent in ingredients, and fancy spices such as rosewater, vanilla, lemon zest were making appearances. However it was only in the 1930s that the US replaced cheese curds with cream cheese, and thus, what we could call the "modern", or New York style cheesecake was born.

Fast forward to the 21st century, cheesecake has boomed in popularity yet again. People just love the creamy, cheesy, sweet decadent dessert. However there are now many ways of making cheesecakes differently than before, so different in fact, one can put these into separate categories of cheesecakes from the New York style. Here we explore the New York, Basque, and the Japanese (cotton) style cheesecake, but this list is by no means comprehensive. It is merely a selection of cheesecakes that the author feels represented the versatility of the dessert well.

To learn more about the world of cheesecakes, head over to the International Cheesecake History Preservation Society (ICHPS).
