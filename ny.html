The classic New York style cheesecake is perhaps what everyone immediately thinks of when they hear the word: "cheesecake". Creamy, Cheesy, Decadent fillings sitting atop a graham cracker crust, this dessert converted many who are skeptical of desserts involving cheese, even those who never really liked cheese before.

The cream cheese and eggs filling makes this cheesecake technically a custard, and makes for an excellent base for all sorts of flavors. Chocolate, cherry, you name it, someone probably has tried to bake it into a cheesecake.

This cake has a few gotchas that makes it somewhat more intimidating for beginner bakers. The requirement for a springform pan and a waterbath makes for a bad combo; this is easily avoided with extra-wide alluminum foil all around (careful! regular 12 inch aluminum foils don't go up the side of the springform pan high enough to prevent leaking). Cheesecakes also love to crack; a good amount of water in the waterbath, and a good distance between the cake and the top heating element usually will help. It also feels like you won't know when the cheesecake is done baking, after all, the thing is like a cream cheese after baking anyway! But this is only a misconception, the toothpick trick will totally work.

Despite these potential difficulties, baking a cheesecake can also be said to be slightly easier. Having to sift flour into a batter, prevent gluten formation while making sure the flours are properly integrated can be a pain for other cakes, but cheesecakes can be flourless! Hoorah!
