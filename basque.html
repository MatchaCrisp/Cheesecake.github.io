When one thinks of desserts in general, "burnt" is probably not a positive descriptor. But this unique "Burnt" cheesecake will surely change your mind. Well, as long as its not too burnt; you want something right at the deeper end of caramelization, where the bitterness kind of sets in. The method to make this kind of cheesecake is almost identical to the NY style, with cream cheese, flour, eggs, and sugar being basically all you need. Notice the absence of the crushed cracker crust, this cheesecake will get its heterogeneity from its naturally crispy outer layer. Baked in a parchment-lined pan, you don't even have to worry about water leaking in because well, this cheesecake won't need it. Simply bake to your desired doneness, ranging from gooey, jiggly, or firm in the middle, wait until it cools and sets, and serve. It's almost impossible to mess up!

The flavor profile of this cheesecake is an instant hit of nutty and bitter crunch from the crust that will form around the filling, immediately followed and balanced by a creamy sweetness that mixes just too well in your mouth. It can be a hard sell on a dinner table, as it looks burnt, but get your guests to take one bite, and they will not be able to stop!
