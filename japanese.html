If you surveyed 100 people ahead of a Family Feud game on characteristics of a cheesecake, "dense" and "decadent" would probably get more than a few votes. And they'd be right, we'd dig our fork in and the cheesecake wouldn't give, when it enters our mouths it would melt like a custard, not bounce like a sponge cake. But what if I told you it is possible to have a cake so fluffy it is jiggly, yet full of creamy and cheesy goodness? Enter the Japanese style cotton cheesecake, viral images and videos of this kind of cheesecake jiggling and bouncing around on a plate are everywhere on the internet. Its secret? Egg whites beaten to glossy, stiff peaks. add that back into the batter with some vanilla, and bake at lower temperatures with a waterbath. What comes out of the oven is a classic looking cake with a golden brown top. The only giveaway is its irresistable aroma of sweet cream cheese, and its unbelievably soft texture as it is moved from pan to plate.

The cake while warm taste like the best part of spongecakes and cheesecakes combined into one delicious and fluffy slice. But even if you had to refrigerate the cake, don't be upset the cake now looks and tastes different. Embrace the fact that there's two right ways to enjoy this dessert! A cold slice of this is like a less dense, somewhat more eggy version of a NY style cheesecake, but with some deliciously flaky crumbs. If you have not been able to try this cheesecake out, we highly encourage you to, we promise you won't be disappointed.
